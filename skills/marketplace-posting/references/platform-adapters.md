# Platform Adapters

Keep first-pass adapters pragmatic. Marketplace pages change, so verify fields visually during browser automation and ask Lucas when unsure.

## Ricardo

Use for valuable, searchable, branded, collectible, vintage, tools, electronics, and quality sets.

Fields to set:

- title
- category
- condition
- description
- photos
- auction or fixed-price mode
- start price / fixed price
- shipping or pickup

Notes:

- Ricardo is suitable for binding auction-style sales.
- Use `sales-strategy` to determine CHF 1 auction vs minimum-bid auction vs fixed price.

## tutti

Use for bulky pickup-only items, furniture, rugs, household goods, tools, concrete box lots, and local fixed-price sales.

Fields to set:

- title
- category
- description
- photos
- fixed price
- location region only
- pickup/shipping note

Notes:

- Keep full address out of public text.
- Expect flaky buyers; clear pickup terms matter.

## Anibis

Use as backup or cross-post for local pickup, especially if tutti does not sell within about 14 days or if broader Swiss/French-speaking reach is useful.

Fields are similar to tutti:

- title
- category
- description
- photos
- fixed price
- location region only

## Vinted

Use for clothing, shoes, accessories, and clothing bundles.

Fields to set:

- category
- brand if known
- size
- condition
- photos
- price
- parcel/shipping options

Stop if size, gender/fit, or condition is missing.

## Facebook Marketplace

Use selectively for fast local pickup and bulky/low-friction items.

Fields to set:

- title
- category
- description
- photos
- fixed price
- general pickup region

Notes:

- Buyer quality may be inconsistent.
- Do not reveal the full address publicly.

## Ron Orp

Use later for design-conscious, aesthetic, or unusual furniture/decor.

Treat as optional/niche until tested.

## AutoScout24

Vehicles only.

Do not post vehicle listings without a separate vehicle checklist.

## eBay

Special-case only.

Use for:

- lightweight specialist collectibles
- rare parts
- model-specific objects
- items with international demand

Avoid as a normal Swiss domestic marketplace.
